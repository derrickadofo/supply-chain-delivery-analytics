# Executive Summary

## Objective
This project analyzed e-commerce delivery performance to identify operational bottlenecks, measure logistics reliability, and assess the effect of delays on customer experience.

## Headline KPIs
- On-Time Delivery Rate: 92.09%
- Late Delivery Rate: 7.91%
- Average Delivery Time: 12.01 days
- Average Delay Time (Late Orders Only): 8.74 days
- Average Review Score: 4.08 / 5

## Key Findings
- Delivery performance is strong overall, but late deliveries remain significant when segmented by geography and seller.
- States such as AL, MA, SE, PI, and CE show late delivery rates far above the network average, indicating geographically concentrated logistics bottlenecks.
- A small group of sellers accounts for disproportionately high late delivery rates, making seller performance management a high-impact intervention area.
- Some product categories show elevated late-delivery risk, but category effects appear less severe than seller and regional effects.
- Delivery delays have a measurable negative effect on customer satisfaction. Orders with lower review scores show much longer delivery times and higher average delay days.

## Recommendations
- Introduce region-specific SLA planning rather than relying on uniform national delivery assumptions.
- Review high-risk sellers and implement targeted performance monitoring or corrective action plans.
- Investigate the handling and fulfillment process for delay-prone product categories.
- Track delivery reliability as a driver of customer experience, not only as an operational KPI.
